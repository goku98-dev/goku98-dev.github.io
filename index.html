Protein–Protein Interaction / Complex Detection via Evolutionary Algorithms

Bioinformatics

An evolutionary-algorithm approach to detecting protein complexes in PPI networks — a core bioinformatics problem. Uses a frequency-matrix chromosome encoding, modularity-based fitness, and Q-guided mutation strategies to cluster proteins into biologically meaningful groups. Achieved 0.90 precision on the Collins PPI dataset.

  • Frequency-matrix encoding for candidate complex sets
  • Three mutation operators: Canonical, Topological, Delta
  • Q-guided mutation for stronger exploration
  • Modularity fitness — maximize intra-cluster density, minimize inter-cluster edges
  • Validated on Yeast D1, Yeast D2, and Collins benchmark networks
